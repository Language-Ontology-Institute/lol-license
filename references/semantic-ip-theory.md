## üìå Preamble: Language Has Changed

With the rise of Large Language Models (LLMs), language is no longer just a communication tool or cultural carrier, but has become a parsable, decomposable, and codable computational resource.

The "semantic structure" of language is being:
- Vectorized
- Subject to agent chaining
- Absorbed internally by models (model embedding)

This makes language no longer just an "expression form" protected by copyright, but something that should be further viewed as orchestratable, computable "semantic capital."

## üèõ Theoretical Reference Basis

### 1. Pragmatics

Speech acts are not merely information transmission, but institutionalized social actions (Searle, Austin).

The tone, rhythm, and narrative logic in semantic modules are replicable "pragmatic devices" and should not be infinitely copied or embedded in AI systems.

### 2. Syntactic Governance

In AI models and agent systems, syntax = operating rules; semantics = control behavior.
Prompts and semantic modules are new "syntactic institutional units."

LOL terms serve as an intellectual property firewall at the syntactic level.

### 3. Extension of Legal Philosophy: Functional IP

When the logical structure of content can be reused, executed, or constitute a functional module, it goes beyond traditional copyright.
Semantic modules should be considered a **new type of intellectual property between code and language**, requiring protection that bridges content law and logic design law.

## üß† Extended Definition: Semantic IP

> Semantic IP refers to language modules, tone structures, and narrative flows that can be structured for use, lead to specific semantic behaviors, and be embedded in AI systems to produce functional effects.

It includes but is not limited to:
- Prompt frameworks and logic modules
- Dialogue structures and tone rhythms
- Modular narrative flows, role dispatch logic
- Semantic chains and control token sequences
